The Curious Case of Benjamin Button

Created: 2021-06-13 | Modified: 2021-07-15

Released in 2008: The Curious Case of Benjamin Button.


Movie about an (old) man who ages backwards. Born as a small, old man. Dies as a baby. All you need to know. You don't get anything valuable from the movie. It's just one of those "feel good" types, I suppose.

I thought that the idea was interesting, but it ended up with many plot holes, and didn't go into his condition as much as I'd like it to. Though, the point of the film was to simply portray what would happen if a person were to age in reverse. It isn't science fiction, so it isn't about the science behind his peculiar condition. I recognize that.

The romantic portions were too much for me, but as I said, I recognize I'm not the target demographic, nor will I be ever be.

I did enjoy the portions during the portrayal of his early life. It eventually got into his teens (in age, not in physical appearance), which is when he started working on a tugboat led by an interesting captain. This captain and he became close friends until he died in the war alongside Benjamin (the main character). As I see it, this captain character was a device used to bring the protagonist into maturity. This was an interesting time in the movie, but things progressively got worse. It's like they forgot that he aged backwards and turned the entire thing into a sappy romance novel, only once again remembering his condition at the very end. It was alright, but not worth the hype. The acting was good, though. No issues with that. Just bad execution of the story.
